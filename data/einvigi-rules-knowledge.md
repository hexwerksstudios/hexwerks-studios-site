# The Raven-Scribe EINVIGI Rules Knowledge

Approved static rules notes curated from the EINVIGI v0.3.6d public rulebook, checked against `EINVIGI-v0.3.6d-public-rulebook-final-public-section-breaks-fixed.pdf`. These notes are for visitor questions only. For full wording, current downloads, and active development discussion, send visitors to the Hexwerks Studios Discord.

## EINVIGI Rules: How the Duel Works

EINVIGI is a one-on-one miniatures duel. Each player commands one warrior inside the hazel-bound ground. There are no units, point costs, terrain rules, weapon ranges, facing arcs, or rulers.

Each round starts with hidden intent. Both players secretly choose one Primary Movement card, one Follow-Up Movement card, and a number of Action Cards based on their fighter's Wounds. Primary Movement and Action Cards are revealed together, legal posture is checked, movement order is determined, Primary Movement resolves, Engagement is checked with the Short Hazel, and then Strike and Grapple are resolved. Follow-Up Movement is revealed after the clash and sets up the next round.

The loop is about commitment and reading the opponent. Strike wounds. Grapple controls position and threatens Ring-Out. Guard holds. Maneuver slips. Oaths earn Wyrd. Momentum rewards rhythm. Overreach punishes bad reads.

## EINVIGI Rules: What You Need

Each player needs one miniature on a 25mm base, one fighter board, Primary Movement cards, Follow-Up Movement cards, Action Cards, Wyrd tokens, Wound markers, Momentum tokens or chits, Overreach tokens or chits, and access to the Short Hazel, Long Hazel, and Crooked Hazel.

The public rulebook also references printable player mats, cards, arena boards, Hazel Staves, and tokens in a separate print-and-play component pack. If a visitor asks for the latest files, send them to the Downloads page or the Discord rather than inventing a file link.

## EINVIGI Rules: Hazel-Bound Ground

The duel is fought in a fixed 9-inch circle made of three concentric zones. The Center Circle is 3 inches across. The Middle Band lies between the 3-inch Center Circle and the 6-inch Middle Circle. The Edge Band lies between the 6-inch Middle Circle and the 9-inch outer hazel boundary. Beyond the outer boundary is Out.

Except when checking Ring-Out, a fighter's zone is determined by the center of their base. Ring-Out uses the separate rule for more than half the base crossing beyond the outer hazel boundary after a Drive.

## EINVIGI Rules: Hazel Staves

Hazel Staves are fixed ritual movement tools, not rulers. The Short Hazel is used for Step In, Step Back, Slip Left, Slip Right, Engagement checking, Drive pushes, Press, Withdraw, and Follow-Up Slip. The Long Hazel is used for Rush. The Crooked Hazel is used for Circle Left, Circle Right, Turn, and Angle positioning.

Players do not pre-measure with Hazel Staves. A Stave is used when a movement option or rule calls for it. EINVIGI does not use model facing. Slip Left and Slip Right are based on the line between the moving fighter and the opponent at the start of movement.

## EINVIGI Rules: Movement

Primary Movement is chosen secretly and revealed with Action Cards. It sets the fighter's legal combat posture for the exchange. Examples include Hold, Step In, Step Back, Rush, Circle Left, Circle Right, Slip Left, and Slip Right. Each posture has requirements for what Action Cards must or must not be revealed.

If a fighter reveals an illegal Action Card set for their Primary Movement, the round is not canceled. That fighter gains Overreach, then the opponent removes the minimum number of illegal Action Cards needed to make the set legal.

Follow-Up Movement is chosen during hidden intent but revealed after Strike and Grapple resolve. It represents recovery, pursuit, withdrawal, or resetting footwork. It does not create a new attack window and does not grant Momentum or Angle.

## EINVIGI Rules: Action Cards

There are four Action Card types: Strike, Grapple, Guard, and Maneuver. Each revealed Action Card gives that pool one die when rolled. Unless modified, action dice succeed on 4+. Rolls of 4, 5, or 6 are successes. Rolls of 1, 2, or 3 are misses.

Strike and Grapple are attacks. Guard and Maneuver are defenses. Strike and Grapple require Engagement. Guard and Maneuver defensive dice are spent after they are rolled.

After Engagement is checked and before attacks resolve, a fighter may spend 1 Momentum for an Optional Feint, converting one revealed Maneuver Action Card into Strike or Grapple. If the converted Strike causes no Wound or the converted Grapple causes no Push, that fighter gains Overreach.

## EINVIGI Rules: Attacks and Defenses

Strike resolves before Grapple. If both fighters Strike, resolve Strike in movement order. If both fighters Grapple, resolve Grapple in movement order. Before an attack resolves, check Engagement. If the attacker is not Engaged, the attack fails. A failed attack with two or more revealed cards in that pool gives the attacker Overreach.

When attacked, the defender chooses Guard or Maneuver if they have unspent dice in that pool. The defender cannot use Guard and Maneuver together against the same attack.

Strike can cause Wounds. Grapple can Push. Push results use Hazel Staves. Drive moves the target straight away with the Short Hazel. Turn moves the target along the Crooked Hazel. Control lets the attacker choose Drive or Turn for each Push.

## EINVIGI Rules: Wyrd Momentum and Overreach

Momentum is spent before an opposed roll to make the fighter's involved pool succeed on 3+ instead of 4+. Overreach is exploited before an opposed roll to make the opponent's involved pool succeed only on 5+. If Momentum and Overreach apply to the same pool, they cancel and that pool succeeds on 4+.

Angle can be spent after a Strike, Grapple, or Guard roll to reroll one missed die from that pool. It cannot reroll Maneuver.

Wyrd is spent after rolling to turn one miss into a success or to break a tied opposed roll in that fighter's favor. Wyrd cannot cancel Wounds, cancel Pushes, move a fighter, or be spent if that fighter already used Momentum or exploited Overreach in the same opposed roll.

Each fighter may use only one of Momentum, Overreach, or Wyrd in each opposed roll. The Momentum cost for Optional Feint does not count against that later opposed-roll resource limit.

## EINVIGI Rules: Wounds and Victory

Wounds reduce how many Action Cards a fighter chooses each round. At 0-1 Wounds, choose 4 Action Cards. At 2-3 Wounds, the fighter is Bloodied and chooses 3 Action Cards. At 4 Wounds, the fighter is Broken and chooses 2 Action Cards. At 5 Wounds, the fighter is Slain and defeated.

Wounds do not reduce Primary Movement or Follow-Up Movement choices.

## EINVIGI Rules: Ring-Out

A fighter is Out if a Drive causes more than half of that fighter's base to cross beyond the 9-inch outer hazel boundary. Only Drive can cause Ring-Out unless a specific rule says otherwise.

Turn can move a fighter within or along the Edge Band, but Turn does not by itself cause Ring-Out. After an opposed roll is fully resolved, check whether any fighter is Out.
