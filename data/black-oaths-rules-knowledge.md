# The Raven-Scribe Black Oaths Rules Knowledge

Approved static rules notes curated from the Black Oaths: Ashes of the North v0.2 source files in the local Norse Warband Skirmish workspace. These notes are for visitor questions only. They are not a full public rules download. For current files, playtest calls, and uncertain rules, send visitors to the Hexwerks Studios Discord.

## Black Oaths Rules: How the Skirmish Works

Black Oaths: Ashes of the North is a grim Viking-age skirmish game of compact warbands, sworn faith, bad omens, and consequences that follow named fighters. A standard game uses two warbands, usually 8 to 12 models each, on a 36 inch by 36 inch battlefield.

Each turn has three phases: Initiative, Activation, and Fate. Players alternate activating one model at a time. Finish one model completely before another model activates: actions, attacks, marker spending, Down or Lost results, carried markers, and activation tracking all resolve before the next model acts.

The main pressure loop is simple: models take actions, attacks use Fate Tests, hits cause Wound Rolls, Wound Rolls place Norn-Marks, make models Down, or make models Lost, and the warband eventually tests Fate when enough bodies or bad omens pile up.

## Black Oaths Rules: What You Need

For a normal game, each player needs a warband, normally 8 to 12 models, a 36 inch by 36 inch battlefield, terrain, several six-sided dice, a ruler or tape measure marked in inches, and markers for Norn-Marks, Wyrd, Down, and any tradition or scenario effects.

The first-game packet recommends dark red or black D6s for Norn-Marks, white, bone, or gold D6s for Wyrd, laying Down models on their side, and using separate markers for scenario pieces such as Lamb, Ember, Hostage, Burn, Claim, or Feud Mark markers.

For a first blind test, the current source recommends a 100-silver game, the 36 inch by 36 inch battlefield, the starter scenario The Last Herd, and the listed first-game warbands rather than the full advanced material.

## Black Oaths Rules: Turn and Activations

Each turn has Initiative Phase, Activation Phase, and Fate Phase.

In the Initiative Phase, resolve start-of-turn effects, determine Initiative, resolve start-of-Initiative effects, then the Initiative player chooses whether to activate first or second. The player with fewer models still on the battlefield has Initiative. If both players have the same number, roll 1D6 and reroll ties.

During the Activation Phase, players alternate activating one model at a time. A model activates only once per turn. If a player runs out of unactivated models, the other player activates their remaining models one by one.

If a Down model activates, it stands first and halves its Move for the rest of that activation.

## Black Oaths Rules: Actions

During its activation, a model can take no more than one of each listed action, in any order: one of Move, Charge, or Withdraw; Scramble; Ranged; Fight; and one special action granted by a rule or scenario.

Move repositions without entering within 1 inch of an enemy. Charge targets a visible enemy within 12 inches, rolls 1D6 plus Move, moves toward the target, and grants a Fight if the model ends within 1 inch. Withdraw lets an engaged model move away, but one eligible enemy may Fight it first. Scramble is a Peril Test for a desperate move. Ranged makes a ranged attack while clear of enemies. Fight makes one melee attack against an enemy within 1 inch. Special actions cover scenario, allegiance, tradition, model, or equipment actions.

Key lockouts: Charge's Fight counts as the model's Fight action. Ranged prevents Charge and Fight in the same activation unless the weapon has Hurl. Heavy ranged attacks prevent movement actions that activation. A carried scenario marker usually prevents Scramble, Ranged, and Charge unless the scenario says otherwise.

## Black Oaths Rules: Fate Tests

A Fate Test is the basic roll for attacks, risky actions, and many rules. Roll 2D6 and read the result: 2-6 is Forsaken, 7-11 is Favored, and 12 or more is Foretold.

Fate Dice and Doom Dice modify the roll. A Fate Die adds an extra die and keeps the two highest. A Doom Die adds an extra die and keeps the two lowest. Fate Dice and Doom Dice cancel each other before rolling.

Before a Fate Test is rolled, the opponent may spend Norn-Marks from the acting model to add Doom Dice, and the model's controller may spend Wyrd to add Fate Dice. Players repeat this spending window until both pass in sequence.

A Peril Test is a Fate Test with a consequence for failure. If the result is Forsaken, resolve the listed consequence and usually end the model's activation.

## Black Oaths Rules: Norn-Marks and Wyrd

Norn-Marks are pressure on a model: fate, blood, fear, exhaustion, curse, or the story closing its hand. A model can have up to 6 Norn-Marks.

An opponent can spend a model's Norn-Marks before that model makes a Fate Test to add Doom Dice. An attacker can spend the target's Norn-Marks before a Wound Roll to add Fate Dice to that Wound Roll.

Wyrd is the model's resistance, favor, nerve, omen, or stubborn place in the story. A model's controller can spend Wyrd before that model makes a Fate Test to add Fate Dice, or before a Wound Roll against that model to add Doom Dice.

Deathblow is a harsher Wound Roll option. When making a Wound Roll against an enemy, spend 3 Norn-Marks from that enemy, or 2 if the target is Down, to roll 3D6 and add all dice instead of making the normal Wound Roll. Wyrd can reduce the Deathblow dice pool to a minimum of 2D6.

## Black Oaths Rules: Combat

Combat has two steps. First make an attack Fate Test to see if the blow, shot, throw, Rite, or Prayer hits. If it hits, make a Wound Roll to see what happens to the target.

For a ranged attack, choose a ranged weapon and visible target in range. If the target is within 1 inch of a friendly model, randomize the actual target before rolling. Cover, long range, and height can change the roll with Doom Dice or Fate Dice.

For a melee attack, choose a melee weapon and an enemy within 1 inch. A second melee weapon can attack once more, but the second attack suffers 1 Doom Die.

Invocations are rare printed Rites and Prayers on specific profiles. They usually use the Ranged action. They are not a generic magic list and cannot be added to models that do not already have one printed on their profile.

## Black Oaths Rules: Wounds Down and Lost

If an attack hits, make a Wound Roll. Fate Dice and Doom Dice can modify the roll, then flat modifiers from armour, Hard-Bitten, Shield, and other rules apply.

The Wound table is: 1 or less is No Harm; 2-7 places 1 Norn-Mark; 8-9 makes the model Down and places 1 Norn-Mark, or 2 Norn-Marks if it was already Down; 10 or more makes the model Lost and removes it from the battlefield.

A Down model suffers 1 Doom Die on Fate Tests, cannot move until it next activates except by falling or forced movement, cannot make free attacks against enemy Withdraw actions, gives melee attacks against it 1 Fate Die on the Wound Roll, does not benefit from Shield, and stands when it next activates.

Lost models are removed from the battlefield. In campaign play, Lost does not always mean clean death, but it does remove the model from the battle.

## Black Oaths Rules: Warband Fate

During the Fate Phase, a warband tests Fate if half or more of its starting models are Down or Lost, rounded up, or if total Norn-Marks on surviving models reaches the threshold for its surviving model count.

A Fate Roll is a Fate Test. A warband adds 1 Fate Die if its Leader is on the battlefield and not Down. A Favored result means the warband holds. A Forsaken result means the warband becomes Doomed. If it is already Doomed and fails again, it breaks and loses.

If a Leader becomes Lost during an activation, test Fate at the end of that activation.

A Doomed warband treats all Fate Tests as Peril Tests. A later successful Fate Roll does not remove Doomed unless a rule says so.

## Black Oaths Rules: Warband Building

For a standard game, build a 100-silver warband. First choose an allegiance: Old Oaths or New Covenant. Then choose a warband tradition.

The current v0.2 structure uses traditions as playable warband packets under the two broad allegiances. Old Oaths covers pagan North, blood rites, sacred groves, seers, jarls, hirdmen, thralls, berserkers, ulfhednar, barrow keepers, and the dead who still have claims. New Covenant covers advancing Christendom, Frankish and Saxon converts, missionaries, priests, relic keepers, martyrs, penitents, bells, books, and sacred law.

Standard limits are 1 Leader, up to 3 Sworn, any number of Levy models, up to 2 Oathbound models unless the tradition says otherwise, and up to 1 Harbinger in a standard 100-silver warband.

Harbingers are rare mythic or supernatural centerpiece models chosen during warband building. They are not summoned mid-game and most warbands should still be mostly mortal warriors.

## Black Oaths Rules: Prophecy Black Oaths and Glory

Every battle can answer three saga questions: what fate has decreed, what the warband swears to do, and how history remembers it.

After choosing a scenario and warbands, but before deployment, players may roll one public Prophecy, each choose one Black Oath, reveal those Oaths unless playing secret Oaths, then deploy and play normally. The first learning game should ignore Prophecies and Black Oaths until players understand activations, Wound Rolls, Norn-Marks, Wyrd, Fate, and scenario scoring.

A Black Oath is the deed a warband swears will make the battle worth remembering. Examples in the current source include Oath of Slaughter, Oath of Vengeance, Oath of the Raven, Oath of Ashes, Oath of the Last King, Oath of the Hanged God, Oath of Broken Shields, Oath of Red Hands, Oath of the Witness, and Oath of Bitter Mercy.

Glory decides who wins. Scenario objectives are the main path to victory, while Black Oaths and Prophecies pull players toward memorable risks. If a warband breaks, it loses immediately and scores no end-of-battle Scenario, Black Oath, or Prophecy Glory unless the scenario says otherwise.

## Black Oaths Rules: Campaigns

Campaign play tracks damaged fighters and warbands across battles. After a battle, resolve injuries for Lost models, award Glory, silver, and scenario rewards, roll for spoils, mark advancement, resolve feuds and recovery, then hire, replace, or retire models.

Lost models roll on an Injury Table after the battle unless the group removes simple Levy or Thrall models from the roster to reduce recordkeeping. Deathblow injuries are worse; recovered bodies may give better odds.

Campaign records can track battles fought, total Glory, Black Oaths fulfilled, Prophecies witnessed, Leaders Lost, and times the warband broke from Doomed.

Models can gain scars, nicknames, advancement marks, Blood Feuds, spoils, cursed trophies, and other consequences. Campaign play should make models more memorable without making them protected from consequence.

## Black Oaths Rules: Traditions and Factions

The current v0.2 tradition packets remain playable as written until a future roster pass rebuilds them into unified allegiance lists.

Old Oaths traditions in the current draft include Vestfold Oar-Kin, Uppsala Hanged Men, Jutland Gate-Keepers, Austvegr River-Kin, Saxon Hold-Clans, and Frisian Tide Folk.

New Covenant currently includes Frankish March Retinues.

Border Oathbreakers are a broken border tradition. Before battle, they choose whether the warband fights as Old Oaths or New Covenant.

Each tradition keeps its own profiles, costs, special rules, reminder card, and table identity. If visitors ask for a full roster, current packet, or exact download, direct them to Discord unless a public website link exists.
